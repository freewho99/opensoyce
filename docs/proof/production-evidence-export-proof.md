From vulnerable-component observation to audit-ready evidence bundle, on live production, in one sitting.

This is a proof artifact, not a marketing claim.

On 2026-06-11, the full trust-decision chain — observation → vulnerability context → remediation question → human decision → expiry pressure → reviewer resolution → evidence export — ran end to end against live production at `www.opensoyce.com`. Real CLI session, real OSV intelligence, real reviewer actions, real reaper run, real database records, and a real export generated by the deployed 17A surface. The thesis it proves:

> OpenSoyce turns component-risk workflows into audit-ready evidence.

The companion artifact is the export itself, verbatim as downloaded from production: [`evidence-bundle-lodash-2026-06-11.md`](./evidence-bundle-lodash-2026-06-11.md) — **all nine sections present, honest-edges `missing: []`**.

## The dogfood doctrine

This chain was purpose-built to be proven quickly. That is allowed — under rules:

```txt
A dogfood record may be purpose-built.
It must still be production-real.
Short expiry is acceptable.
Backdating is not.
DB hacking is not.
The proof is valid when the runtime, schema, events, reviewer
actions, and export all execute normally.
```

Every step below went through the deployed runtime — the published CLI, the production API with session + CSRF, the reaper script against the production database, the production UI. No row was hand-written. No timestamp was invented. The expiry passed because time passed.

## The chain, with timestamps

**1 — Observed (04:59:41Z).** `opensoyce exposure ingest-dependencies --workspace opensoyce --file deps.json` (dry-run first, then real). One `dependency-exposure` record: exposure `65d32e84`, status `observed`, source `cli`, trust boundary `{npm, explicit-json}`. An observation, not a decision.

**2 — Context attached (05:00:44Z).** "Check vulnerability intelligence" asked OSV about `lodash@4.17.20`. The source asserted **five advisories** — `GHSA-35jh-r3h4-6jhm` (Command Injection, high), `GHSA-r5fr-rjxr-66jc` (Code Injection, high), plus three medium — recorded as context rows with severity in the source's vocabulary. Nothing branched on any of them.

**3 — The system asked; the human decided (05:01:11Z).** Remediation question `da53bf51` opened from the high-severity finding (`vulnerability_review`). Answered by `@freewho99`: direction `propose_exception`, reason recorded — upgrade to 4.17.21 scheduled, risk accepted briefly via the exception lane.

**4 — Trust decision (05:01:48Z).** Exception `888aae0f` proposed from the exposure (live-surface proof anchor back at it), then approved BLOCK→WARN by the workspace owner with `expires_at = 05:04:17Z` — a deliberately short window, fully inside the state machine's rules (`expires_at` must be in the future; it was). Both CEI relationship events and both timeline events recorded, with actors.

**5 — Time passed (05:04:17Z).** The exception sat **active-past-window**: review pressure live, nothing yet transitioned. Expiry is time truth, and the truth was now in the past.

**6 — The reaper observed (06:36:32Z).** `node scripts/reap-expired-exceptions.mjs` (dry-run report first, then `--execute`) against production. The guarded transition flipped `active → expired`, touching only the state column — `reviewed_by`, `reviewed_at` (05:01:48Z), `expires_at`, reasons, and anchors all preserved verbatim. The Phase 5 trigger emitted `exception_expired` with a NULL actor; CEI recorded `exception_expired_from_exposure` with `actor: system`. Note the bundle's expiry-pressure section carries BOTH timestamps: *expired at 05:04:17Z · observed by the reaper at 06:36:32Z*. The 92-minute gap is itself evidence — expiry happened when time said so, not when the script ran.

**7 — The reviewer resolved (07:06–07:09Z).** Direction: `remediation_required` — the trust window closed as designed; the remediation path (upgrade to 4.17.21) stands; no renewal. Recorded with reviewer identity and required reason. The exception state after resolving: **still `expired`** — a resolution is a record, not a state transition.

**8 — The export (07:11:29Z).** "Generate evidence bundle" on the exposure detail page. Nine sections, every chain link present, `missing: []`. Downloaded as Markdown from production — that file is the companion artifact, unedited.

## Reproducibility receipt

The bundle was generated twice — once from the production UI (07:11:29Z) and once through the API (07:16:24Z). The two documents are **identical except for the two `generated_at` lines**. Same records, same view: the export adds formatting, never facts.

## Honest edges

- **Two reviewer-resolution records exist for the same expired exception** (`08efc01d` at 07:06Z, `c2d42dd3` at 07:09Z) because the reviewer submitted the resolution twice during dogfood. This is acceptable in the current append-only 16B model and demonstrates the design: resolutions are recorded rather than mutating the exception state, and every prior resolution stays on the record. The export reproduces both, because that is what the record holds.
- The first resolution's reason renders truncated mid-sentence — faithfully: `reason_public` is capped at 280 characters by schema, and the export reproduces what was recorded, not what was meant.
- The proposer and approver are the same person, permitted because they are the workspace owner — and recorded as such. The four-eye rule for non-owner reviewers is enforced by the state machine and is not exercised in this run.
- The reaper required production service credentials and a human at the keyboard. Scheduled, unattended reaping remains deliberately unshipped.
- The long-running natural proof is untouched: exception `b777fb25` still expires 2026-07-10 and will be reaped and resolved on its own clock.

## What this does NOT claim

No SOC 2 compliance, no certification of controls, no proof that lodash was upgraded (a recorded direction is not a completed action — the bundle says this about itself), no auditor replacement, no guarantee of customer security-review acceptance. The export is a faithful view of the record. The auditors decide audit outcomes.

## The proof sentence

OpenSoyce observed a vulnerable component, attached source vulnerability context, asked a remediation question, recorded a human temporary-trust decision, let that trust expire by time, had the system reaper record expiry pressure, had the reviewer resolve the expired trust case, and exported the entire chain as audit-ready evidence — all on production, all through the normal runtime, all in one sitting.
